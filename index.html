DEVScan is a Chrome extension that helps protect users from hyperlink-based threats. We designed DEVScan to analyze links primarily on your device, and to minimize the data that leaves your browser. 1) What DEVScan Accesses (Extension Permissions in Practice)

To provide link safety checks, DEVScanâ€™s content scripts and service worker access:

  • Page Content (DOM): We read page text, hyperlinks, and surrounding markup to identify and annotate links for safety (e.g., highlight, tooltip, help sidebar).
  • Network Requests (link navigation only): We listen for main-frame navigations triggered by links you click or URLs you type, to scan the destination before loading the site and, when necessary, show a warning page.
  • Session State & Preferences (chrome.storage): We store lightweight settings (e.g., strict blocking preference), a short-lived scan session ID, and ephemeral verdict cache entries so we can avoid rescanning the same URL repeatedly for a brief period.

We do not read passwords, form inputs, page content in iframes we donâ€™t inject into, or your clipboard. We donâ€™t record keystrokes or mouse movements.

2) What DEVScan Processes Locally

Most analysis happens locally in your browser:

  • Link & Page Analysis: detect suspicious patterns in URLs and surrounding context.
  • Same-Domain Skips: optionally skip scans for links that keep you on the same domain (configurable).
  • Short-Lived Verdict Cache: a temporary in-memory cache (typically 5â€“10 minutes) so we donâ€™t re-scan the same URL immediately.
  • UI Elements: underline/highlight links, show tooltips/help sidebar, display a warning or scanning page when needed.

These operations are performed in your browser and are not transmitted to us unless a server lookup is necessary (see next section).

3) When DEVScan Contacts Our Server (and What Is Sent)

In limited cases (for example, to check a suspicious URL against our threat models or reputation lists), the extension may call our backend.

Data we may send:

  • URL being evaluated (the link or navigation target).
  • Basic technical metadata needed to operate the service (e.g., a random session ID, extension engine version, high-level browser/extension info string).
  • Optional unshortened URL if the link uses a known shortener (we may request a resolved destination to evaluate actual risk).

Data we do not send:

  • Page contents, your search queries, cookies, account credentials, payment info, or other personal data unrelated to the URL being evaluated.
  • Server logs: As with any web service, our servers may automatically log standard technical information (e.g., IP address, timestamp, user agent) strictly for security, abuse prevention, and service reliability. We do not use this for advertising or profiling.

Retention:

  • URL lookups and verdict responses are processed and returned; any operational logs are retained only as long as necessary for security and reliability, then deleted or aggregated.
  • The verdict cache in your browser expires automatically (generally within 5â€“10 minutes).

4) What DEVScan Does Not Do

  • No keystroke logging or screenshotting.
  • No collection or sale of personally identifiable information (PII).
  • No use of your browsing data for advertising, marketing, or profiling.
  • No third-party ad tech, trackers, or analytics beacons in the extension.

5) How We Use the Information We Access

  • Reduce duplicate scans and improve detection reliability.
  • Operate, secure, and troubleshoot the service.
